DATA USE LETTER

Principal Investigator: Dr. Amara Nwosu, MD, PhD
Institution: Leiden University Medical Center, Department of Human Genetic
Collaborating Institutions: Children's Hospital of Nairobi, University of São Paulo Medical School

1. PROJECT OVERVIEW
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

This project seeks to identify novel pathogenic variants underlying rare, undiagnosed
neurodevelopmental disorders (NDDs) in pediatric patients across three countries:
the Netherlands, Kenya, and Brazil. We will analyze whole-exome sequencing (WES) data
from approximately 800 trios (proband + biological parents).

The cross-national scope is specifically designed to capture the genetic diversity of
NDD presentations across different ancestral backgrounds. All three sites have confirmed
local ethics board approval and national data governance compliance.

2. DATA REQUESTED
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

- Whole-exome sequencing data (trio format where possible)
- Clinical phenotype data: developmental milestones, neurological assessments, MRI findings
- Family history data (three-generation pedigrees)
- No commercial or population-level phenotypic data is requested

3. CONSENT FRAMEWORK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

All participants (or their legal guardians in the case of minor patients) provided
informed consent using standardized consent forms reviewed and approved by each site's
IRB/ethics committee:

- Netherlands: LUMC Ethics Committee, Protocol #LUMC-NDD-2025-019
- Kenya: Nairobi Hospital Ethics & Research Committee, Protocol #NHERC/2025/044
- Brazil: Comitê de Ética em Pesquisa - USP, Protocol #CEP-HU-USP-2025-0217

Consent explicitly covers:
  (a) international data sharing for non-commercial research purposes
  (b) future secondary use for related rare disease research
  (c) possibility of re-contact in case of actionable incidental findings
  (d) storage of samples and data for up to 15 years

Participants were informed about their right to withdraw consent at any time.
A mechanism for withdrawal has been implemented at each site.

Participants were NOT consented for use of their data in commercial product development,
insurance underwriting, or law enforcement.

4. DATA GOVERNANCE AND SECURITY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

This project follows the GA4GH Framework for Responsible Sharing of Genomic and
Health-Related Data. Data transfers comply with GDPR (EU), Kenya Data Protection
Act 2019, and Brazil's LGPD.

All genomic data is pseudonymized at source. A decryption key is held only by the
local site coordinator and is never transferred internationally. Only pseudonymized
data crosses borders.

Data is stored on the ELIXIR federated cloud infrastructure and access is governed
via the GA4GH Passport/Visa system. Role-based access controls are enforced, and
access logs are audited quarterly.

A Data Management Plan (DMP) has been submitted to our funder (EU Horizon Europe,
Grant #HE-2025-RD-4471) and is available upon request.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
5. INCIDENTAL FINDINGS POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Clinically actionable incidental findings (as defined by the ACMG SF v3.2 list) will
be reported back to participants via their local clinical site. Participants were
informed of this policy during the consent process and had the option to opt out
of receiving incidental finding reports.

Non-actionable variants of uncertain significance will not be reported back unless
specifically requested by the participant's clinical team.

6. BENEFIT SHARING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Recognizing the contribution of participants in Kenya and Brazil, we commit to:
- Publishing findings in open-access journals
- Providing capacity-building training to local bioinformatics staff at partner sites
- Ensuring co-authorship for local site investigators on all primary publications
- Contributing variant data to the Global Alliance for Genomics and Health (GA4GH)
  variant databases for the benefit of the broader research community

7. DATA SHARING PLAN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Individual-level data will remain under controlled access and require a formal
data access request to the Data Access Committee (DAC).
